Client Project & Agile Working

A large portion of the course was dedicated to the development of a progressive web application for a real-world client. Our project, No.1 South East, was aimed at delivering a web service that provides its users with offers on food & drink from bars and restaurants across London. The project front-end was developed using the React framework and leveraged Firebase for its backend services using the authentication, databasing, hosting and cloud functions tools it provided. We made use of the Google Distance Matrix API to provide location/distance based sorting of food & drink offers to the user. Personally, I was tasked with developing the admin functionality (primarily admin creation using secure cloud functions), Firestore data creation and data retrieval throughout the web application, sign up functionality, offer code generation, API functionality and other filtering features.

The project was developed using agile principles were individual project features were developed during weekly sprints. We utilised Trello boards and daily stand ups to ensure our productivity was focused and remained high. We also ensured to maintained high levels of communication throughout to prevent duplication of code and accidental breaking of the application during version-control branch merging. Overall, the team worked exceptionally well together and I think we are proud of the product we produced. The practice of agile methods was a good experience and gave us a glimpse into a software development style, we may encounter in our careers but as with all development methodologies it comes with disadvantages which were also good to experience first-hand. In summation the client project has equipped me with the confidence and experience to tackle similar team-based projects in the future.

Airbus

7 year career working primarily within the fuel systems modelling and simluation department. My role involved working within a team responsible for the generation of thermal and spatial models for design, verification & certification (to FAA & EASA CS) of aircraft fuel systems.

Team work & Collaboration

Over the course of my university degree and career at Airbus team work has been the foundation of any work I have been involved in. The scope and complexity of eningeering problems cannot be tackled in isolation, it requires people of different backgrounds and expertise to successfully develop a solution to any problem. No two people think the same and I genuinely believe this is what makes team work fantastic. If we can bring together ideas, thoughts and inspiration from as many people as possible, draw on the knowledge and experience of others all problems should be surmountable.

My group at Airbus was classified as a support function which in essence means we were there to provide assistance to other departments. Therefore my role required me to not just work with my immediate colleagues to design and implement fuel system models but also work closely with all other fuel systems teams; to understand their requirements, constraints, deliver data and analayses that enabled and supported their work. There was no shying away from team work it is the first and foremost skill required from any engineer.

Problem Solving & Innovation

Problem solving, the bread and butter of engineering. Over my career at Airbus I have worked in a plethora of departments (primarily during my graduate scheme) dealing with some very unique and particular problems that require innovative solutions. Modelling, Simulation & Data Analytics

For the majority of my Airbus career I worked in the fuel systems modelling & simulation department. As mentioned my responsibilities involved developing 1D thermal models of the aircraft fuel systems in order to validate the thermal performance (or flammability) of the fuel tanks against the airwothiness authorities (FAA/EASA) certification requirements. The requirements demanded an analysis of the aircraft's flammability on a fleet wide level (or minimum 10,000 flights) and a validation of the model used by comparing it with flight test data. This required the implementation of numerical methods, mainly the monte carlo method, precision in the modelling physics employed and good methods of data handling and analysis. I can confidently say this role developed my knowledge in the aforementioned areas considerably in addition to developing strong coding practices and logic.

The other stream of work I was involved in was the development of the spatial models of the fuel tanks, in which we were tasked with modelling, to a high precision, the fuel tanks volume and inertial characteristics. These models required such high precision as they were required to support fuel system guaging software, flight handling software and even flight simulator software. The fuel tank models were also implemented in further design work and analysis such as the thermal analysis mentioned in the previous paragraph. This work required the implementation of geometrical modelling and mathematical physics and demanded a high level of skill in data handling (i.e. there were huge amounts!). I am proud to say I stepped up to this challenge and became the primary focal point for tank modelling tasks.
